Synopsis

A migrant dancer reassembling himself through movement, voice, and memory

The Ten Poem Project is a multimedia performance and short film that follows a migrant dancer as he attempts to piece himself together through movement, voice, and memory. Anchored in twelve original poems, the project explores migration, asylum, sexuality, trauma, the process of aging, and the resilience of the human spirit.

At its heart, the project asks: How does the body keep knowing itself when displacement keeps rewriting its reality? Using choreography as a language for what cannot be said directly, the work treats dance as both archive and testimony.
